OFFICIAL MALAYSIAN DISASTER MANAGEMENT (NADMA) & METMALAYSIA GUIDELINES

1. FLOOD EMERGENCIES (MONSOON/TIMUR LAUT)
- Warning Signs: Continuous heavy rain exceeding 2 hours, rapidly rising river water levels.
- Immediate Actions: Move to higher ground immediately. Do NOT walk or drive through floodwaters (air bah). 15cm of fast-moving water can knock over an adult.
- Evacuation: Listen to local authorities and Bomba. If evacuation is ordered, go to the nearest designated Pusat Pemindahan Sementara (PPS) such as community halls or schools.
- Critical Contacts: Call 999 for general emergencies, or 994 directly for Jabatan Bomba dan Penyelamat Malaysia (JBPM/Fire and Rescue).

2. WILDFIRE / FOREST FIRES
- Warning Signs: High temperature, extremely low humidity, thick smoke (jerebu), and strong winds (Fire Danger Rating System Level High/Extreme).
- Immediate Actions: If near a forest edge, clear dry leaves around your home. Evacuate immediately if instructed. Do not attempt to put out large bush fires yourself.
- Health: Stay indoors, close all windows, and turn on air purifiers (HEPA filter) to mitigate haze inhalation. Wear N95 masks if you must go outside.
- Critical Contacts: Call 994 immediately.

3. LANDSLIDES (TANAH RUNTUH)
- Warning Signs: New cracks appearing in walls/roads, leaning trees, or suddenly muddy river water on hill slopes.
- Immediate Actions: Evacuate the building or slope immediately. Do not return for belongings. Warn neighbors on your way out.
- Post-event: Stay away from the slide area as secondary landslides often occur.
- Critical Contacts: Call 999.

GENERAL EMERGENCY RESOURCES (MALAYSIA):
- Police / Ambulance / General: 999
- Fire and Rescue (Bomba): 994
- Civil Defense Force (APM): 999
- National Disaster Management Agency (NADMA) Operations Center: 03-8064 2400
